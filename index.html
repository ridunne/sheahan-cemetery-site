Cornelius O. Sheahan

Philadelphia Cemetery Research Project

Introduction

Cornelius O. Sheahan was an Irish immigrant who built a life in Philadelphia during the nineteenth century. Born in Ireland in 1821, he immigrated to the United States and worked as a shoemaker before later becoming a property owner and businessman. His life reflects the experience of many immigrants who came to American cities seeking economic opportunity during the nineteenth century.

Biography

Sheahan lived in Philadelphia where he worked in the boot and shoe trade and eventually invested in property. Census records show him living with his wife Margaret and their son John. Over time his property value increased, demonstrating economic mobility. He lived at locations including Lombard Street and later 502 South 9th Street. Cornelius died in 1902 and was buried at The Woodlands Cemetery.
